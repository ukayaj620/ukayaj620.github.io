2008 Mortgage Crisis

The US mortgage crisis of 2008, also known as the subprime mortgage crisis, was a nationwide financial emergency that contributed to the global GDP recession of 2008â€“2009.

Causes

  • Widespread issuance of subprime mortgages
  • Lack of proper regulation in the financial sector
  • Overvaluation of mortgage-backed securities
  • High risk-taking by banks and investment firms

Key Events

  1. 2006: Housing prices peak and begin to fall
  2. 2007: Rising defaults on subprime mortgages
  3. March 2008: Bear Stearns collapses
  4. September 2008: Lehman Brothers files for bankruptcy
  5. Late 2008: US government passes TARP to stabilize the economy

Consequences

The crisis led to the collapse of major financial institutions, bailouts of banks by national governments, and a global downturn in economic activity. Millions of people lost their homes, jobs, and savings.

Lessons Learned

  • The importance of stricter financial regulations
  • Need for better risk assessment practices
  • Greater transparency in financial products
